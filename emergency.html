Emergency Lines

If you’re in crisis or need urgent help, you’re not alone. Support is just a call or text away.

This page lists emergency and crisis lines specifically chosen with the needs of people with disabilities in mind.

Crisis Lines with Accessibility Options

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

  • 988 Lifeline

  • Dial: 988

  • ASL Access

    • DeafLEAD

  • 24/7 crisis intervention, advocacy, and counseling for people who are Deaf or hard of hearing

  • Video Phone: 321-800-3323

  • DeafLEAD Website

    • Crisis Text Line (Accessible to All)

  • Free, 24/7 support via text from trained crisis counselors.

  • Text HOME to 741741

  • Crisis Text Line Website

    • Disability Rights Hotline (USA)

  • Legal assistance for abuse, neglect, and civil rights violations

  • Call: 1-800-776-5746

  • TTY: 1-800-719-5798

  • National Disability Rights Network

    • Emergency Youth Lines

    Crisis Text Line (Text-Based)

  • For mental health, bullying, disability-related stress, trauma

  • Accessible for the Deaf, Hard of Hearing, non-speaking teens, those with social anxiety

  • Text: HELLO to 741741

    • The Trevor Project (LGBTQ+ Youth)

  • For LGBTQ+ youth in crisis, including those with disabilities or mental health challenges

  • Accessibility: Text, live chat, and trained counselors familiar with overlapping identities

  • Text: Text START to 678678

  • Chat: thetrevorproject.org

    • Teen Line

  • Run by: Trained teen volunteers, supervised by mental health professionals

  • For those who struggle with depression, self-harm, family issues, disability-related stress, social struggles

  • Text: Text TEEN to 839863

  • Chat: teenlineonline.org
