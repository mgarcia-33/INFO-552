Social media can be a powerful space for connection, advocacy, and representation. This section features platforms, creators, and hashtags centered on the disability community aimed to amplify voices, sharing lived experiences, and building community online.

Whether you're looking for support, insight, or inspiration, these channels offer authentic perspectives and meaningful engagement.

Virtual Book Clubs


Chronically Iconic Book Club

  • Focus: Chronic illness & disability representation
  • Format: Monthly read with group chat discussions via Instagram (#chronicallyiconicbookclub)

  • Focus: Disabled readers & books featuring disabled representation
  • Format: Virtual meetings

    • Thrive Book Club

  • Focus: Disability in literature; YA, fiction & nonfiction
  • Format: Ongoing online discussions; occasional Twitter chats and author Q&As (#ThriveBooks on Twitter/Facebook)

  • Focus: Disability equity themes
  • Format: Virtual Zoom meetings with captioning and ASL support

    • Podcasts


    The Accessible Stall

  • Hosted by Emily Ladau and Kyle Khachadurian: (@emily_ladau) and (@ImNotKardashian)
  • Casual conversations and debate about both light and heavy everyday disability topics, between two friends who often have different takes on disability issues.
  • Transcripts included.

    • The Disability Visibility Podcast

  • Hosted by Alice Wong: (@DisVisability)
  • In-depth interviews and discussions with disability community leaders and creators on disability identity, culture, activism and politics, with an emphasis on intersections of disability and race, gender, sexuality, and other marginalized identities.
  • Transcripts included.

    • Barrier Free Futures Podcast

  • Hosted by Bob Kafka
  • Interviews with disability activists and other disabled people in the disability community on current disability issues, history, and experiences.
  • Shows posted after December 2020 have transcripts.

    • Down to the Struts

  • Hosted by Qudsiya Naqui
  • A look at accessible design, both physical and cultural, from a wide variety of angles and voices.
  • Transcripts included.

    • Power Not Pity

  • Hosted by Bri M.: (@powernotpity)
  • Exploring the full range of disability experience, particularly through the stories and work of disabled people of color.
  • Transcripts included.

    • Included: The Disability Equity Podcast

  • Hosted by Bonnielin Swenor, Ph.D. and Nick Reed
  • Discussions and interviews aimed at fighting ableist stereotypes and fully understanding trends in disability issues and policy.
