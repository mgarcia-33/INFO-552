Local Events

Looking to connect with your community IRL?

Our Local Events page features helpful tips and tricks to discover accessible gatherings, workshops, and social meetups that center disability inclusion.

Finding local event is a great opportunity to build friendships, have fun, or to just feel like a brat out in the world! ♥

Check Local Disability Organizations

Start with local disability service providers, independent living centers, or advocacy groups.
These organizations often host or promote:

  • Support groups

  • Adaptive sports leagues

  • Arts programs

  • Workshops and community events

    • Tip: Google “[Your City] + disability center” or “[Your State] + independent living.”

    Use Event Platforms with Filters

    Websites like Eventbrite and Meetup allow you to search by location and use terms like:

  • “Accessible events”

  • “Disability community”

  • “Inclusive meetups”

  • “Wheelchair-friendly events”

    • Tip: Use filters for date, location, and tags to narrow it down

    Check Public Institutions

    Libraries, museums, and community centers often run inclusive programming like:

  • Sensory-friendly exhibit hours

  • Sign language–interpreted performances

  • Inclusive game nights or movie screenings

    • Join Facebook Groups or Discord Servers

    Look for local or identity-specific groups like:

  • “[Your City] Disability Events”

  • “Queer + Disabled Meetups”

  • “Neurodivergent Creatives”

  • “Disability Book Club”
