Resources

It can get difficult to find the information you need and as Charli xcx puts it, "Girl, it's so confusing sometimes to be a girl."

Here, you’ll find disability-focused resources.

Covering everything from accessibility support, mental health, and community programs. Whether you're looking for practical info or ways to connect, this space is built with you in mind.

Therapy & Counseling Services

  • directory that highlights therapists committed to serving marginalized communities, including people with disabilities.

    • Mental Health America (MHA)

  • Includes screening tools, advocacy guides, and disability-centered wellness resources.

    • The Arc

  • Serves people with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD), offering support services and mental health advocacy across the U.S.

    • Psych Hub - Accessible Mental Health Videos

  • Provides explainer videos on anxiety, depression, trauma, and more — with captions, transcripts, and accessible formats.

    • CAST (Center for Applied Special Technology)

  • The founders of Universal Design for Learning (UDL), offering frameworks and tools to make curriculum more inclusive.

    • Bookshare

  • A free resource for students with reading barriers, offering accessible eBooks in audio, Braille, and large font formats.

    • Learning Ally

  • Provides audiobooks and reading tools for students with dyslexia or visual impairments, especially for K-12 and college students.

    • Office for Civil Rights (OCR)

  • Protects students from discrimination in schools and colleges. Students and parents can file complaints regarding accessibility and accommodations.

    • National Disability Rights Network (NDRN)

  • A legal advocacy network with state partners to support students facing barriers in public education.

    • National Disability Rights Network (NDRN)

  • A legal advocacy network with state partners to support students facing barriers in public education.

    • RespectAbility

  • Focuses on inclusive education and leadership development for disabled students, especially BIPOC and LGBTQIA+ youth.

    • Autistic Self Advocacy Network (ASAN)

  • Offers plain-language guides and toolkits for self-advocacy in education settings.

    • Peer Support Space

  • A peer-led organization offering virtual and in-person support groups, including those centered on disability and neurodivergence.

    • Sins Invalid

  • A disability justice-based performance project that explores mental wellness through art, culture, and identity.

    • Autistic Women & Nonbinary Network (AWN)

  • Provides community, resources, and mental health support with a focus on autistic people who are women, nonbinary, and/or gender nonconforming. Emphasizes lived experience and peer support.

    • Easterseals Project Action

  • Provides resources to improve accessible transportation and mobility across the U.S.

    • Transit Planning 4 All

  • Advocates for inclusive, community-driven public transportation planning for older adults and people with disabilities.

    • Centers for Independent Living (CILs)

  • Community-based organizations run by and for people with disabilities, offering peer support, advocacy, and services to increase independence.

    • National Resource Center on Supportive Housing and Home Modification

  • Offers guidance on making homes safer and more accessible, especially for older adults and people with physical disabilities.
